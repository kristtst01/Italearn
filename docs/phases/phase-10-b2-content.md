# Phase 10 — B2 Content & Fluency

**Goal:** Complete the curriculum with B2 (Sections 9-10, Units 31-38). The learner reaches upper intermediate — passato remoto, indirect speech, advanced pronoun usage, register, idioms. The interface shifts predominantly to Italian.

## What This Delivers

- Sections 9-10 fully populated (8 units of content)
- Checkpoint 9 and Final Checkpoint
- Interface language shift to predominantly Italian
- Idiomatic expression and register exercises
- ~4,000 cumulative word families
- The full A1→B2 curriculum is complete

## Learning Principles Addressed

- **L1→L2 transition complete**: by B2, the interface and grammar explanations are predominantly in Italian, with English only for genuinely complex grammar points. This follows the research-backed gradual transition (Macaro 2005) and builds immersion at the level where learners can handle it.
- **Register awareness**: real fluency requires knowing when to use tu vs Lei, formal vs informal vocabulary, written vs spoken constructions. Unit 36 explicitly teaches this — something Duolingo barely touches.
- **Idiomatic competence**: B2 learners need to move beyond grammatically correct but stilted Italian into natural expression. Unit 37 teaches high-frequency idioms in context, with contrastive notes showing the English equivalent.
- **Passato remoto**: essential for reading Italian literature, news, and understanding Southern Italian speech. Often skipped or delayed too long in apps.

## Content to Generate

### Section 9: Refinement (Units 31-34)
- Passato remoto (regular: parlai, parlasti, parlò... + key irregulars: fui, ebbi, feci, dissi, vidi, venni, volli)
- Indirect speech (discorso indiretto) — tense shifting rules, reporting verbs (dire che, chiedere se, spiegare che)
- Gerund/infinitive/participle constructions (pur sapendo, prima di partire, una volta arrivato)
- Advanced pronominal verbs (farcela, andarsene, cavarsela, prendersela, avercela con)
- Deep dive into ne and ci beyond basic usage

### Section 10: Fluency (Units 35-38)
- Advanced conjunctions requiring subjunctive: affinché, nonostante, a meno che, purché, sebbene, benché
- Register and formality: Lei forms revisited, formal correspondence conventions, congiuntivo as marker of formal speech
- Idiomatic expressions: in bocca al lupo, non vedo l'ora, prendere in giro, fare il furbo, avere le mani in pasta, non c'entrare niente, dare una mano
- Proverbs: chi dorme non piglia pesci, tra il dire e il fare c'è di mezzo il mare
- False friends comprehensive review: actually/attualmente, argument/argomento, eventually/eventualmente, pretend/pretendere
- Final consolidation: longer reading passages, extended dictation, full grammar review

## Technical Deliverables

### 1. Interface Language Switch
- Settings option: "Interface language" — English / Mixed / Italian
- At B2 start, prompt user: "You've reached B2! Want to switch the interface to Italian?"
- UI strings (button labels, navigation, instructions) available in both languages
- Exercise instructions in Italian (e.g., "Traduci in italiano" instead of "Translate to Italian")
- Can be switched back at any time

### 2. Register Exercise Type
- Present a scenario (e.g., "You're writing to your professor" vs "You're texting a friend")
- User chooses the appropriate phrasing from options, or rewrites a sentence in the correct register
- Tests tu/Lei, formal/informal vocabulary, written/spoken conventions

### 3. Idiom Context Exercise
- Present an Italian idiom in a sentence with context
- User guesses meaning from context (multiple choice)
- Then: use the idiom in a new sentence (free-form production)
- Shows English equivalent and literal translation for comparison

### 4. Final Checkpoint
- Comprehensive review spanning all A1→B2 material
- Heavier on production than any previous checkpoint
- Includes reading passage, dictation, free-form writing, and speaking
- Completion unlocks "B2 Complete" badge — the end of the structured curriculum
